bio

Just a quick introduction of myself: I'm Kevin, a sophomore EECS (electrical engineering & computer science) major at University of California, Berkeley! I first touched programming in early high school, when I dabbled in Microsoft Visual Basic and C++. That quickly developed into studying Java for the Oracle certification in junior year (unfortunately, the rules for the certification changed and required a hefty fee).

In July 2011, when DreamHost had a $9.99 July 4th web hosting deal, I started working on my own non-profit website called Paw for Paw (I'm a big Siberian husky guy) and got to play around with HTML/CSS and some Javascript, as well as visit some slobbering pups at the local Humane Society shelter.

Now, I'm working my way through the little nooks and crannies behind computer science theory, learning things beyond my wildest imagination (you know, solving currency conversion problems using Dijkstra's algorithm, making reductions from one NP-complete problem to another). Along the way, I like to delve into some side projects, including this website that I set up from scratch while learning HTML/CSS two days ago.

Some of my hobbies include soccer (over 11 years and counting), guitar (still a learning beginner), reading books and news, and board games (Scrabble, anyone?).
